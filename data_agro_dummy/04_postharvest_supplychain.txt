# Chapter 4 — Post-Harvest Handling and Supply Chain Basics

## 4.1 Why post-harvest matters
For many vegetables, post-harvest losses can be as damaging as field pests. Heat, bruising, and dehydration reduce quality and market price.

## 4.2 The “3 rules”
1) Harvest at the right maturity.
2) Remove field heat quickly (cooling).
3) Protect from crushing and moisture loss during transport.

## 4.3 Handling lettuce (highly perishable)
Main risks:
- Wilting from water loss
- Browning and decay if too warm
- Crushing in transport

Best practice workflow:
- Harvest early morning when leaves are cool and turgid.
- Shade immediately; avoid leaving crates in the sun.
- Pre-cool if possible (forced-air cooling or cold room).
- Use clean, ventilated crates; avoid overfilling.
- Maintain airflow in the truck; do not block vents.
- If no cold chain is available: shorten time to market, use wet burlap on crates (not soaking), and transport at night if feasible.

## 4.4 Packaging and stacking
- Strong crates prevent compression damage.
- Stack in a stable pattern; avoid tall unstable stacks.
- Use liners for delicate produce, but ensure ventilation.

## 4.5 Simple quality grading
Separate produce into:
A) premium (best price),
B) standard (normal market),
C) processing/animal feed (avoid mixing with A).
This improves overall revenue and avoids reputation damage.

## 4.6 Traceability (simple)
Write on each batch:
- farm name, date, crop, field section
If a complaint happens, you can identify which field and harvest day had issues.

## 4.7 Transport checklist
- Clean truck bed
- Shade or cover from direct sun
- Secure crates to avoid sliding
- Keep produce away from chemicals and fuel odors
